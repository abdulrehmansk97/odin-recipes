Kanda Poha

Description

Kanda Poha is a popular and traditional breakfast dish from Maharashtra, India. It is made from flattened rice (poha) cooked with onions, green chilies, mustard seeds, turmeric, and garnished with fresh coriander and lemon juice. This dish is light, flavorful, and perfect for a quick and healthy breakfast or snack.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups poha (flattened rice)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Optional: roasted peanuts for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse the poha: Place the poha in a colander and rinse it under running water until it becomes soft. Let it drain completely.
  2. Heat oil: In a large pan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add curry leaves and chilies: Add the curry leaves and chopped green chilies to the pan. Sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Add onions: Add the finely chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn translucent.
  5. Add turmeric: Add the turmeric powder and sauté for another minute.
  6. Add poha: Add the softened poha to the pan. Mix well to ensure the poha is evenly coated with the spices.
  7. Add seasoning: Add salt and sugar (if using) to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the poha is heated through.
  8. Garnish and serve: Turn off the heat and garnish the poha with chopped coriander leaves and roasted peanuts (if using). Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
